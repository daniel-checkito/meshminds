YOUR FIRST
7 DAYS

Exactly what to do each day. Check them off as you go. By Day 7 you have a live listing.

1DAY
Pick ONE product. Not two. One.
Use the selector above. Read that product's full breakdown. Understand exactly what you're printing and why people buy it.
2DAY
Print one test unit. Photograph it properly.
Natural light. Clean white background. Hold the product in your hand for one photo. That personal touch converts. Avoid renders — Etsy's 2025 policy requires real photos.
3DAY
Use the prompt from this guide to write your listing.
Paste the prompt into Claude or ChatGPT. Edit the output. Write your first Etsy title, description, and 13 tags. Done in 20 minutes.
4DAY
Open your Etsy shop. List the product.
Price at 5× material cost minimum. Use the calculator above. If you feel like the price is "too high" — it is probably correct. Underpricing is the #1 mistake.
5DAY
Share in 2–3 Facebook groups for your niche.
Not "buy my product" posts. Show the product, tell the story, include your Etsy link naturally. Example: "Made this for my own desk, listing it now if anyone wants one."
6DAY
Print 5 more units. Improve based on questions.
Any question someone asks about your product is a gap in your listing. Answer it in the description. Questions = free market research.
7DAY
Review: did anyone buy? Here is what to do.
Yes → Print more, respond fast, ask for a review. No → Change the main photo first (that is the biggest conversion lever). Then check your title. Price is the last thing to change.
Read This Before You List

5 MISTAKES THAT
KILL NEW SELLERS

These are the exact mistakes that cause 80% of new shops to make zero sales in month one.

MISTAKE 01
Pricing on filament cost only
You spend €2 on filament and charge €5. After Etsy fees (40%), electricity, machine wear, packaging, and your time — you made €0.20 and spent 2 hours. You are working for less than minimum wage.
✅ Fix: Use the calculator above. Price at 5× real cost minimum. If it feels too expensive — it is probably correct.
MISTAKE 02
Using renders instead of real photos
Etsy's 2025 Creativity Standards require real photos of the physical item. Shops using renders or stock images are getting flagged. Real photos also convert 3× better than renders.
✅ Fix: Print the product. Photograph it in natural light. Hold it in your hand for one photo. Takes 20 minutes. Converts infinitely better.
MISTAKE 03
Selling licensed characters
Pokemon, Disney, Marvel, Warhammer — selling prints of licensed characters is trademark infringement even if you designed it from scratch. Games Workshop has a dedicated legal team searching Etsy. One DMCA notice can delete your entire shop including all reviews.
✅ Fix: Original designs only. If you use a licensed STL file from Hive by Cubee, that is covered. Anything else — design it yourself.
MISTAKE 04
Not drying filament before printing
PETG absorbs moisture from the air within 24 hours of opening. Wet filament causes bubbling, stringing, and failed prints that look like settings errors. Most sellers spend hours adjusting settings when the real fix costs €15 and 4 hours in a food dehydrator.
✅ Fix: Dry PETG for 4–6 hours at 65°C in a food dehydrator before printing. Store in vacuum bags with silica gel.
MISTAKE 05
Listing everything instead of one thing
New sellers list 20 different products hoping something sticks. Etsy's algorithm rewards shops with consistent niche focus. A shop with 5 listings in one niche outranks a shop with 50 listings in 10 different categories.
✅ Fix: Pick one product. Get 10 reviews on it. Then add one more product in the same niche. Build depth, not width.
